Ahrum-Stock Guitar The Ahrum-Stock Guitar is a digital artwork created using the borkfest palette from Lospec. The artist spent approximately 8 hours working on this piece, carefully crafting each pixel to bring the guitar to life. The canvas size of the artwork is 278 x 371 pixels, which may appear somewhat messy due to the unconventional dimensions. One of the unique aspects of this artwork is the technique the artist refers to as "beautiful noise." Rather than meticulously placing each pixel in a perfect pattern, the artist embraces randomness and allows for imperfections in the placement of pixels within a chosen area. This technique adds a sense of authenticity and realism to the artwork, reminiscent of the subtle variations and irregularities found in real-life objects. By embracing the randomness and imperfections, the artist captures the essence of organic creation, adding depth and character to the Ahrum-Stock Guitar. This technique not only showcases the artist's skill and creativity but also creates a unique visual experience for the viewers. The beautiful noise brings an element of surprise and unpredictability, enhancing the overall aesthetic appeal of the artwork.

The artwork also incorporates dirty and disruptive noise, which further contributes to the overall atmosphere. The noise adds a gritty and textured quality, suggesting turmoil or conflicting emotions within Ahrum's psyche. This inversion creates a stark contrast and disorienting effect, making the viewer question the familiar and challenging their perception. The use of inverse colors enhances the sense of depth and adds a surreal quality to the artwork. Another notable aspect is the large array of palette colors and the artist's choice of colors. The extensive color range allows for a nuanced representation of Ahrum's psyche, highlighting various emotional states or facets of their personality. The choice of colors adds richness and complexity to the artwork, enabling the viewer to explore different layers of Ahrum's inner world. By combining the techniques of "jaggies," dirty and disruptive noise, inverse colors, and a diverse color palette, the artist creates a multidimensional representation of Ahrum's psyche. The artwork invites the viewer to delve into the depths of Ahrum's mind, evoking a sense of curiosity, intrigue, and emotional connection.

"See Evil Speak Evil Hear Evil" is a digital artwork created using a custom "Zeph Hook" palette. The artist dedicated approximately 5.2 hours to complete this piece. The artwork explores the theme of evil, focusing on the senses of sight, speech, and hearing. One prominent technique employed in this artwork is the use of polygons and polygonal shapes. Instead of utilizing traditional organic forms, the artist opts for geometric shapes to convey the subject matter. The polygons bring a sense of structure and angularity to the artwork, evoking a feeling of rigidity or sharpness that aligns with the concept of evil. The artist also incorporates the use of noise to enhance the visual impact. By adding controlled and deliberate noise, the artwork gains texture and complexity. This technique introduces subtle variations and irregularities, contributing to the overall aesthetic appeal and giving the piece a sense of life and vitality. The limited color palette chosen for this artwork further adds to its unique character. By restricting the colors used, the artist creates a sense of focus and intentionality. This limitation forces the artist to carefully select the colors that best represent the theme of evil and the specific elements within the artwork. The limited color palette adds a sense of cohesion and enhances the overall impact of the piece. Through the combination of polygons, polygonal shapes, noise, and a limited color palette, the artist successfully captures the essence of evil and its association with the senses of sight, speech, and hearing. The deliberate use of these techniques creates a visually intriguing and thought-provoking piece. The angular forms and limited color palette evoke a sense of uneasiness, while the noise and polygons add depth and texture, contributing to the overall impact and interpretation of the artwork.

"Ahrum Getting Eaten" is a digital artwork created with a custom handcrafted palette. The artist dedicated approximately 3 hours to bring this piece to life. The artwork depicts a scene where Ahrum, presumably a character or subject, is being consumed or devoured by something. One notable technique employed in this artwork is referred to as "extreme shading" or "disruptive shading." Instead of utilizing a traditional shading approach that gradually transitions from dark to light tones, the artist opts for a more dramatic effect. In this technique, the darker tones are rendered even darker, while the lighter tones are made even lighter. This deliberate exaggeration creates a stark contrast between the shadows and highlights, resulting in a visually striking and impactful image. By eliminating the middle ground or the use of medium tones, the artist achieves a heightened sense of contrast and intensity in the artwork. This approach adds a sense of depth and dimension, amplifying the overall impact of the shading. The extreme shading technique creates a distinct visual style, capturing the viewer's attention and evoking a strong emotional response. Through the deliberate manipulation of light and shadow, the artist accentuates the dramatic nature of the scene, emphasizing the act of Ahrum getting eaten. The extreme contrast in shading adds a sense of urgency or tension, intensifying the viewer's engagement with the artwork.
